About Me

My name is Ali, and I am a Computer Engineering student at the Arab Academy for Science and Technology, Cairo branch. I am passionate about hardware design, embedded systems, and the intersection between software and electronics.

Throughout my studies, I have worked on several projects involving microcontrollers (ATmega32), UART communication, timers, LCDs, and keypads. One of my main projects was a security lock system using two ATmega32 microcontrollers, where I handled both control logic and HMI implementation. This project helped me strengthen my understanding of embedded C programming, debugging, and system-level design.

In addition to embedded systems, I am currently studying Data Structures and Algorithms, improving my problem-solving and programming efficiency in C and Python. I enjoy learning how to write optimized and structured code that can be applied in real-world systems.

Outside of academics, I am very interested in technology, gaming, and computer hardware. I like exploring how hardware and software can work together efficiently, whether it’s in engineering systems or high-performance gaming setups.

My goal is to build a strong foundation in computer engineering and work on innovative embedded or system-level projects that push my skills further and prepare me for a professional engineering career.
